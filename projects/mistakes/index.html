The Honesty Penalty
Overview Results Model
Hypothesis The costlier the mistake, the more it should be hidden.

Everyone slips up. Owning up lets the damage be fixed; the whole team gains.
But the one who confesses takes the blame alone.
The more money a mistake burns, the angrier the manager: bigger stakes, harsher punishment for owning up.
Workers see that coming: the higher the stakes, the more they should hide.
The twist being tested: silence should peak exactly when honesty is worth most.

Results Only hypothetical stakes ever mattered.

Workers hid a third of their mistakes; with a bigger punishment stick, 43%.
Raising the stakes changed nothing: not the hiding, not the punishment. Tested twice, real money.
Only the hypothetical version behaved: on paper, judges cut the confessor’s bonus harder when more was at stake.
The catch in the lab: whoever owns up alone eats nearly all the punishment; the quiet teammate walks away.
And practice makes it worse: hiding rises round after round.
