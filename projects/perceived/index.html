An asymmetry. Clients respond to the fee, complying more, rewarding activity and punishing bold moves, but advisors do not. The price paid changes the buyer’s behaviour while leaving the seller’s advice untouched.
Advisor side Higher fees move none of the three.

The three pre-registered advisor outcomes, each measured under the low fee (£0.35) and the high fee (£1.75), computed from the experiment data (Round 1). The estimates are statistically indistinguishable across the two fee levels: paying the advisor five times as much shifts nothing.

Client side The fee does move the client (exploratory).

Away from the pre-registered advisor tests, the fee leaves clear traces on the client’s behaviour. These effects are exploratory, computed from the experiment data (Round 1); the retention comparisons use the REP treatment, in which clients could fire their advisor.

Comply more
Clients follow expensive advice more closely, moving further towards a recommendation when they have paid the high fee for it.
Reward activity
Clients prefer advisors who advise more often, rating and retaining frequent advisers more favourably.
Punish big moves
Clients sanction advisors who suggest large changes, penalising bold departures from their first guess.
