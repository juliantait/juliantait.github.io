Hypothesis We expect higher fees to push advisors to signal more value.
  • Once paid, the flat fee is sunk — standard theory says it should not affect any decision that follows.
  • Still, we expect higher fees to raise the pressure to be seen as giving value for money.
  • Viewing time is fixed, so effort cannot help — advisors can only signal on the margins they control.
  • Signal on frequency: advise more often (H1a).
  • Signal on boldness: recommend bolder shifts (H1b).
  • Quality should be no better under the high fee (H2).
Advisor side Higher fees don’t change advisor behaviour

The three pre-registered advisor outcomes, each measured under the low fee (£0.35) and the high fee (£1.75), computed from the experiment data (Round 1). The estimates are statistically indistinguishable across the two fee levels: paying the advisor five times as much shifts nothing.

Client side Clients reward frequency and punish boldness.
