What each sender maximises

Every sender picks the message m — share the trait (m=t) or stay silent (m=∅) — to maximise this utility. The display reacts live: each mechanism button below switches its part of the equation on or off, and while a mechanism is on its scale slider appears beneath the buttons.

The paper's main mechanism. A shared view is group-diagnostic, so the sender bears the reputational inference cost ci(1−δ̂(gi|m)) with sensitivity ci∼Exp; the slider sets the scale E[ci] (canonical value 1). Switch it off to remove identity inference entirely (ci→0). With the interaction cost active this recovers pure pluralistic ignorance: silence is driven only by the anticipated chance of meeting the opposing type, S0g,t = Ee[Φ((e(1−ρt)−μv)/σv)], and no longer depends on group identity. Switch it back on and the unprotected majority reappears. A second, encounter channel on top of identity inference, à la Bursztyn et al. (2020): voicing a view risks meeting someone who disagrees. A sender who shares meets one person — an opponent with probability 1−ρt, where ρt = μAπt,A + μBπt,B is the marginal prevalence of the view — at an individual encounter disutility ei∼Exp, drawn independently of (vi, ci). Utility gains the term −ei(1−ρt) on sharing, so the silence rule becomes m=t ⇔ vi > ei(1−ρt) + ciΔLg,t. This splits the Bursztyn et al. social-image payoff into an intrinsic encounter cost (about your view) and the reputational inference cost (about your group). A stereotyping observer, Bordalo et al. (2016), who over-reads a representative trait: likelihoods become πstt,g ∝ πt,gR(t,g)θ and the stereotyped posterior δ̂st replaces Bayes in the loss differential. Switching on sets θ = 0.4, the Bordalo et al. estimate; θ = 0 is pure Bayes.

Primitive distributions

Canonical model. Heterogeneity in both primitives — expressive value vi∼N(μv,σv2) and inference sensitivity ci∼Exp(1) (Bayesian observer); solves the Perfect Bayesian Equilibrium silence rule m=t ⇔ vi > ciΔLg,t numerically. ▸

The population primitives behind every solve: expressive value vi∼N(μv,σv2), inference sensitivity ci∼Exp (scale on the identity-inference slider above), encounter disutility ei∼Exp (interaction-cost slider), and the group prior μ = Pr(A).

Who stays silent, within each group

Each group is shown as 100 people, split by trait (icon colour). People who stay silent in equilibrium are faded ghost icons — so the faded share reads straight off as a percentage. Switch to Bars for the per-cell silence rates. Icon styling after Bordalo et al. (2016).

Equilibrium summary & tables

The underlying numbers behind the figures above.

Silence rate Sg,t — share of each cell that withholds.

Loss differential ΔLg,t = δ̂(g|∅) − δ̂(g|t).

Naive disclosure posteriors δ̂(g|t) — naive-observer belief after a shared trait.

Silence posterior δ̂(g|∅) — equilibrium belief after silence.
